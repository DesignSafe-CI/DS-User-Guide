# Curation &amp; Publication User Guide

Below are step-by-step guides on how to create projects in the Data Depot, and curate and publish work/data across DesignSafe. We offer the following project types when publishing: Experimental, Simulation, Hybrid Simulation, Field Research, and Other. More information on Data Depot policies, project types, and curation/publication can be found at:

* <a href="/rw/user-guides/curating-publishing-projects/policies/">Data Depot Repository (DDR) Policies</a></li>
* <a href="/rw/user-guides/curating-publishing-projects/best-practices/">Best Practices</a></li>
* <a href="https://www.designsafe-ci.org/rw/user-guides/curating-publishing-projects/faq/">Curation &amp; Publication FAQs</a>.</li>

### 1. Add a Project</h3>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/24/e1/24e1f7ee-eda6-497c-be92-e50a05ba15cc/1.jpg__203x185_q85_subsampling-2.jpg"

</p>

</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>You can start a project at the very beginning of its lifespan, upload and curate data incrementally, then publish sets of data at your convenience.</p>

<p>To add a new project, click <strong>+ Add</strong>, then select <strong>New Project</strong>.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/13/fe/13fe768f-e9cd-42b3-99f3-847aa6dd9da1/12_add_new_project.png__442x277_q85_subsampling-2.png"
    
    
    
    
>








</p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>In the Add New Project window, fill in a project title and list the principal investigator (PI) and project members.</p>

<p>PIs and project members have the same level of access to the project, but the PI will be listed separately in the metadata. Additional PIs will be listed as Co-PIs. </p>

<p>You can edit all these fields later if you make any mistakes.</p>

<p>Once finished, click <strong>+</strong> <strong>Add Project</strong> and you will be taken to your new project in the My Projects tab.</p>

### 2. Add an Experiment</h3>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/f4/db/f4dbe805-b85e-4f25-b05e-6a560218342b/21_curation_directory.png__442x149_q85_subsampling-2.png"

</p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>To begin curation and add an experiment, click on the <strong>Curation Directory</strong> and select <strong>Experimental</strong> as your Project Type.</p>

<p>Go through the overview and fill out additional required fields in the <strong>Edit Project</strong> window, click <strong>Update Project, </strong>then click <strong>Add Experiments</strong>.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/c1/95/c19594b1-447b-4191-871f-e3c11af4690c/22_add_experiment.png__375x418_q85_subsampling-2.png"
    
    
    
    
>








</p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>Adding an experiment involves filling out high level information about the files you will be publishing. Each experiment will receive its own DOI. Add multiple experiments if any of this information changes or you want multiple DOIs. Do not add another experiment if you are testing multiple models and this information stays the same. Instead, you can add multiple models to one experiment.</p>

<p>Fill out the required and optional fields using the advice given to create concise and understandable descriptions. </p>

<p>Also, assign authorship from a list of the project members and PIs. You can order the authors later when it is time to publish.</p>

<p>Click <strong>+</strong> <strong>Add Experiment</strong> when you are done and it will appear below in your inventory.</p>

<p>You can edit an experiment from the inventory.</p>

### 3. Add Categories</h3>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/39/6b/396b7d9d-1c09-46e3-8187-d7dff53d593b/experimental_add_categories.png__477x433_q85_subsampling-2.png"
    
    
    
    
>








</p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>Click <strong>Add Categories</strong> to begin.</p>

<p>Categories group files together based on a shared purpose in an experiment. Be sure and read the definitions of each category to understand what files belong to each.</p>

<p><span style="color: #145ec2;"><strong>Model Configuration</strong></span> Files describing the design and layout of what is being tested (some call this a specimen).</p>

<p><strong><span style="color: #3b9b92;">Sensor Information</span></strong> Files about the sensor instrumentation used in a model configuration to conduct one or more event.</p>

<p><span style="color: #ac8804;"><strong>Event</strong></span> Files from unique occurrences during which data are generated.</p>

<p><strong><span style="color: #4cb8db;">Analysis</span></strong> Tables, graphs, visualizations, Jupyter Notebooks, or other representations of the results.</p>

<p><strong>Report</strong> Written accounts made to convey information about an entire project or experiment.</p>

<p>After filling out the fields, click <strong>+ Add Category</strong> and it will appear below in your inventory. If you make any mistakes, expand the category and click <strong>Edit</strong>.</p>
</div>
</div>

### 8. Prepare to Publish</h3>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/3a/ad/3aadf4d3-b87f-458a-a3c3-a91d7fcad4af/81_prepare_to_publish.png__418x112_q85_subsampling-2.png"
    
    
    
    
>








</p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>When you are satisfied with how your work is curated and wish to publish it, select Prepare to Publish in the Publication Preview. </p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-sm-6">
<center>
<p>







<img class='img-responsive' src="/media/filer_public_thumbnails/filer_public/d5/cb/d5cb572c-4546-4988-8441-f03691dacd81/82_licenses.png__418x410_q85_subsampling-2.png"
    
    
    
    
>








</p>
</center>

<center>
<p> </p>
</center>
</div>

<div class="col-sm-6">
<p>There are 6 stages in the publication process: <br>
Selection, Proofread Project, Proofread Experiment, Proofread Categories, Order Authors, and Licenses</p>

<p>In <strong>Selection</strong>, select which experiment you want to publish. You can only publish one at a time.</p>

<p>In <strong>Proofread Project</strong>,<strong> Experiment</strong>,<strong> &amp; Categories</strong>, take time to proofread all the descriptions and metadata you have entered. You cannot make changes after publishing.</p>

<p>In <strong>Order Authors</strong>, order the authors of the experiment and preview how your citation will appear.</p>

<p>In <strong>Licenses</strong>, select one or more licenses that best fit your data.</p>

<p>Finally, click <strong>Request DOI &amp; Publish</strong> and agree to the agreement to publish your work.</p>
</div>
</div>

### 2. Add a Mission</h3>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">To begin curation, click on the <strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'>Curation Directory</span></strong> and select <strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'>Field Research</span></strong> as your Project Type.</span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">A mission is a group of data collections that are associated with a common goal or time. Some researchers may also refer to this as Time or Wave.</span></span></span></p>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">Adding a mission involves filling out high-level information (e.g. title, date, geolocation and description) about the files you will be publishing. </span></span></span></span></p>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">Each mission will receive a unique DOI. <p><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">A group of files related to planning and logistics, study design and administration, design, Institutional Review Board (IRB) procedures, or permits.</span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">A group of related data and associated materials from the engineering/geosciences domain.</span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">A group of related data and associated materials from the social sciences domain.</span></span></span></p>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">When you are satisfied with how your work is curated and wish to publish it, select Prepare to Publish in the Publication Preview. </span></span></span></span></p>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">There are 6 stages in the publication process:<br>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">In <strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'>Selection</span></strong>, select which mission you want to publish. At this time, you can only publish one mission at a time.</span></span></span></span></p>

<p style="margin-top: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-bottom: 7.5pt; margin-left: 0in;"><span style="background: white;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt;"><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'><span style="color: #333333;">In <strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'>Proofread Project</span></strong>,<strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'> Missions</span></strong>,<strong><span style='font-family: "Helvetica Neue";'> &amp; Collections</span></strong>, take time to read all the descriptions and metadata you have entered.
